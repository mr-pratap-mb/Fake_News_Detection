Project Overview & Motive

In the era of rapid digital communication, misinformation spreads faster than the truth. Traditional fake news detectors rely entirely on pre-trained "static datasets." This means if a new hoax was invented yesterday, standard AI models won't detect it because it wasn't in their training data.

Our Motive: We built FakeShield v2 to solve the "Static Data Problem." Our primary motive was to create an intelligent system that verifies claims exactly like a human journalist would—by actively searching the live internet for evidence, checking the credibility of those sources, analyzing the mathematical stance, and presenting a transparent verdict.

Core Features & Pipeline

1. Live OSINT Web Corroboration

FakeShield breaks down the user's input, extracts the core nouns, and dynamically scrapes the open web (via NewsAPI) for matching evidence. If a claim has no digital footprint, the system automatically flags it as unverified.

2. Deep Semantic Filtering & NLTK

We implemented Natural Language Toolkit (NLTK) to analyze Topological Parts of Speech (like Nouns/Verbs) and Numerical bounds. If the claim says "150 years", the live evidence must natively contain the number "150" or the article is discarded to prevent AI hallucinations.

3. Stance Detection & Sensationalism Matrix

The ML model doesn't just look for "Fake-sounding" words. It analyzes the ratio of sensational superlatives. Furthermore, if the OSINT evidence explicitly contains words like "hoax" or "debunked", FakeShield forcefully flips the probability matrix to Fake.

4. Triggers, Heatmaps & The Contrast Engine

The UI generates a visual Heatmap locating specifically categorized forensic flags (Linguistic Tricks, Logical Fallacies). Finally, The "Contrast Engine" runs a silent background query targeting official Fact-Checking sites (Snopes, AltNews). If found, a glowing Red Shield Alert is displayed bridging direct proof to the user.
